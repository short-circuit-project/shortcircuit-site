About

The person behind
the project

Short Circuit is built by one person learning AI the hard way — by doing real things with it and telling the truth about how it goes.

Hi, I'm Rich.

I spent the better part of a decade in manufacturing operations and strategy. Supply chains, production lines, quality systems, global expansion — the whole world of making physical things at scale. I worked my way from operations roles to the board of directors of an electronics manufacturer, overseeing international expansion and capital allocation.

And then I hit a wall.

The work stopped being interesting. The problems felt recycled. I was good at my job, but I wasn't excited about it anymore. And when AI tools started getting genuinely useful — not "novelty toy" useful but "this changes how work gets done" useful — I realized I had a choice: watch from the sidelines, or dive in headfirst and see what happens.

I chose headfirst.

Short Circuit started as a learning project. I gave myself a simple rule: learn AI by building real things with it, and document every step publicly. No courses. No certifications. Just hands-on projects, a weekly newsletter, video content, and radical transparency about what works and what doesn't.

The co-pilot for all of this is Claude, Anthropic's AI. Every project, every newsletter issue, every piece of this website — built through conversation with an AI. I prompt. We iterate. Things break. We fix them. I learn something. I write about it. Repeat.

That's Short Circuit. One person, one AI, real projects, no faking it.

If you're curious about AI but don't know where to start, or you're an operations professional wondering how this stuff applies to your world — you're who I'm building this for. Subscribe to the newsletter, follow along on TikTok, and watch what happens when a curious person with zero AI background decides to figure it out live.
